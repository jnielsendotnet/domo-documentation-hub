### Intro

Domo supports single sign-on (SSO) using two protocols: Security Assertion Markup Language 2.0 (SAML) and OpenID Connect (OIDC). Both SAML and OIDC can operate concurrently.

This article describes how to choose the best protocol—SAML or OIDC—for your use case and how to configure SSO with your chosen protocol.

Depending on your configuration, SSO can also auto-provision Domo users and assign them their user role, attributes, and group membership.

<Tip>
**SCIM:** In addition to SSO, Domo also supports user and group management via SCIM. Learn [more](/s/article/000005241).
</Tip>

---

### Required Grants

You must have the Admin system role or a custom role with the Manage All Company Settings grant enabled to set up SSO. Learn more about [system](/s/article/360043438953) and [custom](/s/article/360043438973) roles.

### Access SSO Configuration Settings

You can find Domo SSO configuration settings by navigating to **Admin** > **Authentication** > **Single Sign-On (SSO)**.

### SAML vs. OIDC: Choose a Protocol

Domo supports both SAML and OIDC. Choosing which one to use depends on a few considerations. All other things being equal, OIDC is the more modern standard and a good choice. For Domo setup, management, and functionality, consider these differences:

- OIDC may be easier to configure, especially if your IdP provides a well-known config.
- SAML requires certificate exchange and management, which introduces some overhead.

Additionally, you may want to ask the following:

- Does your organization dictate which you should use?
- Is your team more familiar with one or the other?

<Note>
**Use SAML and OIDC concurrently:** You may have a case for using both SAML and OIDC concurrently. For example, your employees may use one IdP, and contractors may use another. In this case, you can integrate using SAML for one IdP and OIDC for the other.

You may also want to use one protocol as the default for logging into Domo and the other protocol as the default for accessing embedded content.
</Note>

### Direct Sign-On List

When you enforce the Direct Sign-on List, only the users on that list and users with the Admin system role log on to Domo directly with Domo credentials. All other users must sign in through SSO. To prevent direct sign-on for all users except those with the Admin system role, enforce the Direct Sign-on List and leave it empty. To enable direct sign-on for specific users, add those users to the Direct Sign-on List.

<Note>**Note:** Users with the Domo Admin system role can always sign in to Domo with their Domo credentials, regardless of whether the Direct Sign-on List is enforced or whether they appear on the list.</Note>

# Enforce the Direct Sign-On List

# Manage the Direct Sign-On List

You can manage the Direct Sign-on List in Domo by navigating to **Admin** > **Authentication** > **Single Sign-On (SSO)**> **Direct Sign-On List**(tab).

Here, you can add or remove users from the list one at a time or in bulk. When adding users to the list, you can optionally include notes to display in the list.

<Frame>![Screenshot](/images/kb/0EMVu00000TfhQ5.jpg)</Frame>

<Warning>**Important:** Depending on your SSO and Direct Sign-on List configuration, the Domo auth screen may either not display or may not give the option to log into Domo using Domo credentials. In this state, users on the Direct Sign-on List and users with the Admin system role can log into Domo using Domo credentials by going to: `https://{YourSubdomain}.domo.com/auth/index?domoManualLogin=true`</Warning>
