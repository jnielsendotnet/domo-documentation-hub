## Intro

Google Ad Manager helps you manage ad revenue by streamlining operations and maximizing the value of each impression. Use the Domo Google Ad Manager connector to access data about ad units, companies, and orders as you manage digital ad revenue across screens, formats, and sales channels.

When you bring your Google Ad Manager data into Domo, you can view real-time, interactive dashboards. You can access these dashboards from your phone, tablet, or computer, and receive alerts when key metrics change. You can also combine your Ad Manager data with data from other sources to get a comprehensive view of your business.

You can connect to your Google Ad Manager REST account in the Data Center. This topic discusses the fields and menus that are specific to the Google Ad Manager REST connector user interface. To add DataSets, set update schedules, and edit DataSet information, see [Adding a DataSet Using a Connector](/s/article/360042926274).

## Prerequisites

To connect to your Google Ad Manager REST account and create a DataSet, you must have a Google account with a username and password.

## Connecting to Your Google Ad Manager REST Account

This section enumerates the options in the **Credentials** and **Details** panes on the Google Ad Manager REST Connector page. The components of the other panes on this page, **Scheduling**, and **Name & Describe Your DataSet**, are universal across most connector types and are discussed in great detail in [Adding a DataSet Using a Connector](/s/article/360042926274).

## Credentials Pane

The Google Ad Manager REST connector uses OAuth to authenticate, so you don’t need to enter credentials in Domo. Select **Connect** (or select **Add Account** if you already have Google Ad Manager REST accounts in Domo) to open the Google OAuth page, where you can enter your Google credentials.

Once you have entered valid Google Ad Manager REST credentials, you can use the same account any time to create a new Google Ad Manager REST DataSet. You can manage connector accounts in the **Accounts** tab in the Data Center. For more information about this tab, see [Manage Connector Accounts](/s/article/360042926054).
