## Intro

[Particle.io](http://Particle.io) is a manufacturer of Internet-connected hardware and microcontrollers. Particle allows developers, hardware engineers, and DIY makers to build Internet-connected devices via WiFi, Cellular and Mesh connectivity. To learn more about the Particle API, visit their page (https://docs.particle.io/reference/device-cloud/api/).

You connect to your Particle account in the Data Center. This topic discusses the fields and menus that are specific to the Particle connector user interface. General information for adding DataSets, setting update schedules, and editing DataSet information is discussed in [Adding a DataSet Using a Data Connector](/s/article/360042926274 "Adding a DataSet Using a Data Connector").

## Prerequisites

To connect to your Particle account and create a DataSet, you must have a Particle access token. For help generating a token, please consult the [Particle.io API documentation](https://docs.particle.io/reference/device-cloud/api/#generate-an-access-token "https://docs.particle.io/reference/device-cloud/api/#generate-an-access-token").

## Connecting to Your Particle Account

This section enumerates the options in the **Credentials** and **Details** panes in the Particle Connector page. The components of the other panes in this page, **Scheduling** and **Name & Describe Your DataSet**, are universal across most connector types and are discussed in greater length in [Adding a DataSet Using a Data Connector](/s/article/360042926274 "Adding a DataSet Using a Data Connector").
