## Intro

Faceted bar charts allow you to combine many charts into one and save the space of replicating the axis.

<Frame><img alt="faceted_bar.png" src="/images/kb/ka05w00000123la-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EM5w000005vP2T.png"/></Frame>
## Powering Faceted Bar Charts

The faceted bar chart requires a Category, a Value and a Series.

For information about value, category, and series data, see [Understanding Chart Data](/s/article/360043428693).

In the Analyzer, you choose the columns containing the data for your faceted bar chart. For more information about choosing data columns, see [Applying DataSet Columns to Your Chart](/s/article/360043428713).

For more information about formatting charts in the Analyzer, see [Visualization Card Building Part 2: The Analyzer](/s/topic/0TO5w000000ZaoUGAS).



## Customizing Faceted Bar Charts

You can customize the appearance of a faceted bar chart by editing its **Chart Properties**. For information about all chart properties, see [Chart Properties](/s/topic/0TO5w000000ZaodGAC "Chart Properties").

Unique properties of faceted bar charts include the following:

| Property                             | Description                          | Example                                                                                                  |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| General > Percent Space Between Bars | Determines space between bar charts. | <img alt="percent_space_bars.png" src="/images/kb/ka05w00000123la-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EM5w000005vP2V.png"/> |
