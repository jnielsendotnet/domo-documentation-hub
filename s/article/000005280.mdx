### Intro

Consumption pricing is a way of paying for Domo where organizations are billed based on their overall usage, rather than charged for a predetermined number of licenses. This gives organizations unlimited access to our platform—user seats are free, and no products sit behind a paywall.

To use Domo with the consumption model, organizations purchase a pool of credits that will support their usage over the period of their contract (typically 1–3 years).

This article describes [what credits are](#what_are_credits) and [how they are consumed.](#how_are_credits_consumed) You can see the [Consumption Terms](https://www.domo.com/consumption-terms) for more information.

We have created a number of tools to help you monitor and confidently use your credits. To learn more, see [Credit Monitoring](/s/article/000005326).

### What Are Credits?

Consumption credits are a standard unit of usage. They are like a currency within Domo that organizations purchase and consume by using any and all of what the platform offers.

### How Are Credits Consumed?

Credits are consumed by using Domo. Organizations have access to all features and the credits they purchase are consumed as they use the features. Generally, all features and functionality in Domo are powered by storage (data rows under management in Domo) and compute (executions performed on that data).

The number of credits consumed by each function varies. See the [Consumption Terms](https://www.domo.com/consumption-terms) for more detailed information.

To learn about tracking your credit consumption, see [Credit Monitoring](/s/article/000005326).
