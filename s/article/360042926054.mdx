### Intro

Accounts is where you manage the details for connector integrations. In the Accounts interface, you can do the following:

* View and manage your own accounts and any accounts shared with you
* Transfer DataSets to others' accounts or change the accounts associated with DataSets
* Share connector accounts with others so they can also create DataSets using that connector

<Warning>
  **Important:** If you delete someone from Domo and reassign their DataSets to someone else, the permissions for the DataSet accounts are not passed to the new owner. Ownership of each account must be transferred manually. For more information, see [Remove Users from Domo](/s/article/360042934334 "Removing Users from Domo").
</Warning>

### Add an Account

You can add an account to Domo by doing one of the following:

* Add a DataSet to Domo and enter new account information (as opposed to selecting an existing account). After adding an account by this method, the listing displays in the **Accounts** interface. Learn more in [Adding a DataSet Using a Connector](/s/article/360042926274 "Connecting to Data with Connectors").
* Add an account from the **Accounts** interface. Learn how below.

Follow these steps to add an account from the **Accounts** interface:

1. From the **Accounts** interface, select **Add New** > **Account**.
     
   The **Add Account** modal displays.

   <Note>
     **Note:** You can use the **Assign Somone** option in the modal to choose someone else in your organization to connect their account.

     <Frame><img alt="Screenshot 2024-11-25 at 2.20.43 PM.png" src="/images/kb/ka0Vq0000005Erx-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EMVq0000040twh.jpg" style={{width: 400, height: 244}}/></Frame>
   </Note>

2. From the dropdown, select the connector.
     
   <Frame><img alt="Screenshot 2024-06-20 at 7.30.33 PM.png" src="/images/kb/ka0Vq0000005Erx-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EMVq000001RQXJ.jpg" style={{width: 499, height: 558}}/></Frame>

4. Select **Connect**.
5. Select **Done**.

An entry for the new account appears in the **Accounts** interface.

### Rename an Account

When you create accounts, either by adding them in the Accounts interface or entering credentials for a new DataSet, those accounts are automatically assigned the connector name. For example, if you add an account for Facebook, that account is called "Facebook Account." Other accounts you add for Facebook are also called "Facebook Account." This can make it confusing to differentiate between accounts for the same connector. You can eliminate this confusion by giving unique names to accounts in the **Accounts** tab.

Follow these steps to rename an account:

1. In the accounts list, hover over the account you want to rename.
2. Select
   <img src="/images/kb/ka0Vq0000005Erx-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EMVq0000017Xuj.jpg" style={{width: 20, height: 20, display: 'inline', verticalAlign: 'start', margin: '0'}}/> **Account options** > **Edit account**.
3. In the modal, hover over the account name until the edit field displays.
     
   <Frame><img alt="Screenshot 2024-06-20 at 7.11.48 PM.png" src="/images/kb/ka0Vq0000005Erx-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EMVq000001RQCL.jpg" style={{width: 497, height: 236}}/></Frame>
4. Enter the new name and select **Connect** to keep the new name OR **Close** to discard the change.

### Share a Connector Account

You can share connector accounts with others so they can access all available data via this integration and use it to create new DataSets. You can choose the level of access each person/group receives to the integration itself.

This can include giving a person or group Owner-level access to an account.

Accounts shared with you appear in the accounts list, but they are grayed out. You can only use shared accounts to access the system through the connector.

Follow these steps to share an account:

1. In the accounts list, hover over the account you want to share.
2. Select
   <img src="/images/kb/ka0Vq0000005Erx-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EMVq0000017Xuj.jpg" style={{width: 20, height: 20, display: 'inline', verticalAlign: 'start', margin: '0'}}/> **Account options** > **Share account** to display the **Manage Account access** modal.
3. In the modal, use the search tool to find people/groups or scroll through instance members to find them.
4. Use the access menu beside a person or group name to choose their level of access. The options are as follows:

   * **Owner —** Allows editing, sharing, and deletion of the integration. There can be multiple account owners.
   * **Can Edit —** Allows editing and sharing the integration, but not deletion.
   * **Can Share —** Allows sharing, but not editing or deletion of the integration.
   * **Can Read —** Allows integration use, but not sharing, editing, or deletion of the integration.
   * **No Access —** Denies access to the integration.
       
     <Frame><img alt="manage account access.jpg" src="/images/kb/ka0Vq0000005Erx-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EMVq0000017daM.jpg" style={{width: 697, height: 619}}/></Frame>
     After being granted access, the person/group displays in the modal under **People With Access**.
5. (Conditional) Repeat steps 3–4 as many times as needed to give people and groups the proper access.
6. Select **Apply** to display the confirmation dialog. Note the impact of granting access.
7. In the dialog, select **Apply** to save your changes and close the modal OR select **Cancel** to discard your changes.

### Revoke Account Access

You can revoke access to an account for people/groups. When access is revoked, the person/group can no longer access the associated DataSets.

Follow these steps to revoke account access:

1. In the accounts list, hover over the account to which you want to revoke access.
2. Select
   <img src="/images/kb/ka0Vq0000005Erx-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EMVq0000017Xuj.jpg" style={{width: 20, height: 20, display: 'inline', verticalAlign: 'start', margin: '0'}}/> **Account options** > **Share account** to display the **Manage Account access** modal.
3. In the **People With Access** section of the modal, locate the person/group for whom you want to revoke access and expand their access dropdown.
4. Choose **No Access**. **Note:** There must be one account owner. If someone is the only owner, you cannot change their access until you add another owner.
     
   <Frame><img alt="revoke access.jpg" src="/images/kb/ka0Vq0000005Erx-00N5w00000Ri7BU-0EMVq0000017hfa.jpg" style={{width: 698, height: 622}}/></Frame>
5. (Conditional) Repeat steps 3–4 as many times as needed.
6. Select **Apply** to display the confirmation dialog. Note the impact of revoking access.
7. In the dialog, select **Apply** to save your changes and close the modal OR select **Cancel** to discard your changes.
