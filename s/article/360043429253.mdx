## Intro

A Running Total Line chart is similar to a standard Line chart, except that each successive data point in a series line displays the cumulative total (or "running total") of all data points in that line up to that point. Although you can use any kind of categorical data in a Running Total chart, typically, you use date or date-time data. Other chart types in Domo that utilize this same "running total" concept include [Running Total Bar charts](http://wiki.domo.com/confluence/display/DHCS/Building+a+Running+Total+Bar+graph "Running Total Bar Graph"), which include one series bar per category; [Running Total Grouped Bar charts](http://wiki.domo.com/confluence/display/DHCS/Building+a+Running+Total+Grouped+Bar+graph "Running Total Grouped Bar Graph"), in which series bars in a category are shown side by side; and [Running Total Stacked Bar charts](http://wiki.domo.com/confluence/display/DHCS/Building+a+Running+Total+Stacked+Bar+graph "Running Total Stacked Bar Graph"), in which series bars in a category are stacked on top of each other.

## Powering Running Total Line charts

A Running Total Line chart requires at least two data columns or rows from your DataSet—one for categories and one for values. Series columns or rows are optional. Each series in your DataSet appears as an additional line in your chart. For information about value, category, and series data, see [Understanding Chart Data](/s/article/360043428693 "Understanding Chart Data").

In the Analyzer, you choose the columns containing the data for your Running Total Line chart. For more information about choosing data columns, see [Applying DataSet Columns to Your Chart](/s/article/360043428713 "Applying DataSet Columns to Your Chart").

For more information about formatting charts in the Analyzer, see [Visualization Card Building Part 2: The Analyzer](/s/article/360043428673 "KPI Card Building Part 2: The Card Builder").
